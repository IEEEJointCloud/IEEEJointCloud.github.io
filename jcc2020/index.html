Basic Introduction to JCC

  • What is JCC?

    JointCloud Computing (JCC) is a new cloud computing paradigm - it is also referred to as Hybrid-Cloud, Inter-Cloud, Multi-Cloud, Cloud Federation, Supercloud, Cross-Cloud and Cloud Service Broker) etc. JointCloud computing supports bilateral or multilateral collaboration among various cloud participants (such as providers, consumers, agents, regulators, etc.) at different levels of traditional cloud (such as IaaS, PaaS, SaaS, DaaS, etc.) in a mutually beneficial manner to meet the major challenges facing the development of cloud computing.

Friendship Links

IEEE JCC 2020 will be co-located with five other IEEE conferences:

  • The 14th IEEE International Symposium on Service Oriented System Engineering (IEEE SOSE)
  • The 8th IEEE International Conference on Mobile Cloud Computing, Services, and Engineering (IEEE Mobile Cloud)
  • The 6th IEEE International Conference on Big Data Computing Service and Applications (IEEE BigDataService)
  • The 2nd IEEE International Conference on Artificial Intelligence Testing (IEEE AITest)
  • The 2nd IEEE International Conference on Decentralized Applications and Infrastructures (IEEE Dapps)

All five conferences will follow a common daily schedule and share some events such as keynote talks and panel discussions.

The joint conferences program will take place from April 13 until April 16, 2020.
